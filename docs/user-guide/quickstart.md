# Quick Start

Get started with Species Explorer in just a few minutes!

## Step 1: Open Species Explorer

Click the Species Explorer icon in the toolbar, or go to **Plugins** → **Species Explorer** → **Species Explorer**.

![Open Plugin](../assets/open-plugin.png)

## Step 2: Search for a Species

1. Enter a species name in the search box (e.g., "Panthera leo" or "lion")
2. Click the **Find** button
3. A list of matching species will appear

![Search Species](../assets/search-species.png)

!!! tip "Search Tips"
    - Use scientific names for more accurate results
    - Partial names work too (e.g., "Panthera")
    - Common names are supported for many species

## Step 3: Select a Species

1. Click on a species in the results list
2. The taxonomy information will be displayed
3. Review the species details to confirm your selection

![Select Species](../assets/select-species.png)

## Step 4: Fetch Occurrence Data

1. Click the **Fetch** button
2. Wait for the data to download from GBIF
3. A new point layer will be added to your project

![Fetch Data](../assets/fetch-data.png)

!!! info "Download Time"
    Large datasets may take longer to download. The status bar shows progress.

## Step 5: Explore the Data

Your new layer includes:

- **Point locations** for each occurrence record
- **Attribute table** with detailed information
- **Default styling** with clustered visualization

![Explore Data](../assets/explore-data.png)

## Example: African Lion

Let's explore the distribution of the African Lion (*Panthera leo*):

1. Search for "Panthera leo"
2. Select the species from results
3. Click Fetch
4. View the occurrence points across Africa

The result shows museum specimens, field observations, and citizen science records from across the species' range.
